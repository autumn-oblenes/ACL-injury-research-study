About the Research

Understanding the impact of footwear and playing surface on ACL injuries in soccer

The Problem

ACL injuries are a major growing concern in women's soccer affecting not just professional players but also many teenage players. There is still limited large-scale, player-reported data examining how modifiable factors such as footwear type, stud type and playing surface influence injury risk, particularly in teenage athletes.

Our Goal

This study aims to collect a large volume of self reported data to examine these factors and how they may interact in affecting ACL injury risk in this age group.

Why Participate

It takes two minutes, is completely anonymous and you would be contributing to knowledge that will hopefully change the future of ACL risk in teenage female soccer players.

Consent to Participate in Research
Under 18 - Parental Consent

You are invited to participate in a research study examining the relationship between playing surface, footwear type, and the risk of ACL (anterior cruciate ligament) injuries in female soccer players.

If you are under the age of 18, a parent or legal guardian must review this information and provide consent before you participate.

Parent/Guardian Consent:

Your child is being invited to take part in a voluntary, anonymous survey that will take approximately 3–5 minutes to complete. The survey includes questions about soccer playing history, playing surfaces (e.g., grass vs. turf), footwear, and any history of ACL injury.

No personally identifying information will be collected. All responses will remain anonymous and confidential. The information gathered may be used for research purposes, including presentations or publications, with the goal of improving injury prevention in soccer.

Participation is completely voluntary. Your child may stop the survey at any time without any consequences.

By allowing your child to proceed with the survey, you confirm that:

  • You are the parent or legal guardian of the participant
  • You have read and understand the information above
  • You give permission for your child to participate in this study

Participant Assent (for players under 18):

By continuing with the survey, you confirm that:

  • You understand what the study is about
  • You agree to participate voluntarily
  • You understand that you can stop at any time

If you have any questions about this study, please contact: autumnoblenes2027@gmail.com

Thank you for your support and participation.
